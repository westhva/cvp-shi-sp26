🚀 Email Templates — POTENTIAL & NEW Accounts

SHI × Canva Spring 2026 Blitz  ·  Low or zero usage — educate first, earn the conversation

Strategy for this tier

These contacts haven’t adopted Canva yet. Your job with email is to earn a conversation — not close a deal. Lead with their operational pain, not product features. The goal of Email 1 is a reply or a booked call. Price urgency and “there’s going to be a price increase” messaging is irrelevant here — they’re not using the product. Focus on workflow efficiency and the cost of the status quo.

🚫 What NOT to say to this tier: Don’t mention price increases. Don’t list features. Don’t open with “Canva is a design platform that…” — open with their problem instead.

📩 Recommended Sequence

Send these in order. Stop when they respond.
Email 1 — Pain hook → Email 2 — Workflow efficiency → Email 3 — Enterprise proof → Email 4 — Breakup
  • Space emails 3–4 business days apart
  • If they reply at any point — stop the sequence and respond directly
  • Always personalise [highlighted fields] before sending
Email 1 — Pain Hook

Subject: How does your team create content today?

To: Marketing manager, ops lead, department head, or anyone who owns content creation
Goal: Ask a question that makes them think. Get a reply.
Hi [Name], Quick question — when someone on your team needs a presentation, a sales asset, or a marketing piece and your design team is busy, what usually happens? They wait. Or they make something that goes out off-brand. Or the designer gets pulled away from higher-value work. Most organisations have this problem and assume it’s just how it works. It doesn’t have to be. I’d love to share what some similar organisations are doing to fix this — would you be open to a 20-minute call this week? [Your Name] SHI | [Phone]
💡 The question in the opening line does the work — it makes them picture the problem before you’ve described any solution. Don’t mention Canva by name in Email 1 if you can help it.
Email 2 — Workflow Efficiency

Subject: What if every team could create on-brand content?

Send: 3–4 days after Email 1 (no reply)
Goal: Make the value concrete with a specific use case
Hi [Name], Following up on my note earlier this week. Here’s the core idea I wanted to share: what if your sales team could update their own decks? Your regional managers could create their own event materials? Your HR team could produce their own onboarding docs? All locked to your brand. No design review. No back-and-forth. That’s what Canva Enterprise is built to do — give every team in your org the ability to create professional, on-brand content without touching a designer. Your design team sets the guardrails once, then gets out of the bottleneck permanently. Most companies think of Canva as a design tool. Enterprise is an operations tool. Worth a quick conversation to see if it fits? [Your Name] SHI | [Phone]
💡 “Enterprise is an operations tool” — this reframe works well because it separates the conversation from “another design subscription” and positions it alongside productivity and ops software.

Email 4 — Breakup

Subject: Closing the loop, [Name]

Send: 4–5 days after Email 3 (no reply)
Goal: Get a yes, no, or “not yet” — all three are useful
Hi [Name], I’ve reached out a few times about how other organisations are fixing their content creation bottlenecks — want to close the loop. If it’s not the right time, completely fine — just say the word and I’ll check back in later. If you’re curious at all, I can keep it to 15 minutes. Either way, happy to hear from you. [Your Name] SHI | [Phone]
💡 Short, low pressure. The explicit “just say the word” permission to decline almost always gets a response — even if it’s just a “not right now”, which is a useful data point.
