🔥 Phone Script — HOT & WARM Accounts

SHI × Canva Spring 2026 Blitz  ·  These teams are already using Canva — convert shadow IT

Strategy for this tier

These accounts have existing Canva usage — HOT with paid seats, WARM with high free usage. Don’t sell Canva, sell the upgrade. Canva Business is the easiest first step: quick decision, low friction, gets it off personal credit cards. If the conversation opens into workflows and brand management, push for Enterprise.

Shadow IT exists → 💳 Canva Business (fast close) → 📈 Enterprise (if workflows open up)

📄 Before You Call

  • Check YAU and paid seat count in the dashboard — these tell you HOW engaged they are
  • HOT = already paying out of pocket. WARM = heavy free usage. Both are warm calls.
  • Target: IT manager, procurement lead, or whoever manages software spend
  • You have a built-in opener — their team is already using the product

📞 Opening

Say this “Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] from SHI. I’m your account rep, and I’m reaching out because I can see your organisation already has people actively using Canva — and I wanted to talk about the quickest way to formalise that through SHI before it becomes a problem. Do you have a couple of minutes?”
“Before it becomes a problem” opens the shadow IT conversation naturally — uncategorised software spend is a real pain for IT and procurement.

💳 Lead: Canva Business (the easy win)

⚡ HOT accounts — already paying “Right now your team has paid Canva seats that are probably sitting outside your normal software spend — personal credit cards, expensed subscriptions. That’s common, and it’s easy to fix.

The quickest path is Canva Business — 25 seats, currently priced just under $5,000 total. Most IT teams can approve that on a card same day, no procurement needed. It gets all those seats consolidated under one SHI invoice, with proper billing and visibility.

We can have that sorted in days rather than weeks. Does that kind of quick win make sense?”
🌊 WARM accounts — heavy free usage “Your team is clearly getting value from Canva — which is great. The issue is they’re hitting the ceiling on what the free tier can do: no brand controls, no admin visibility, no premium content.

The fastest way to unlock all of that is Canva Business — 25 seats for just under $5,000. Credit card purchase, no procurement needed, and your team gets premium features immediately.

Does that sound like something worth a quick conversation?”

📈 Pivot: Canva Enterprise (when the conversation opens)

Listen for these signals: “we have lots of teams”, “brand consistency is a problem”, “IT needs control”, “we use Adobe/other tools”. These are Enterprise conversations.

📈 Pivot to Enterprise “Actually, based on what you’re describing — multiple teams, brand consistency challenges, IT needing visibility — it sounds like what you might really need is Canva Enterprise rather than Business.

Enterprise is built for organisations, not just individuals. It adds SSO and SCIM provisioning, brand kit controls that lock down templates and fonts org-wide, admin dashboards, approval workflows, and SOC 2 compliance.

The way I’d think about it: Canva Business is great for a team. Enterprise is for an organisation that needs governance at scale. Which sounds more like your situation?”
🔍 SPICED discovery questions (Enterprise path)
  • Situation: How many teams are currently using Canva across your org?
  • Pain: What challenges do you have with brand consistency or content quality today?
  • Impact: If any team could create on-brand content without going through a designer — what would that save you?
  • Critical Event: Any upcoming campaigns or launches where this would matter most?
  • Decision: Beyond price, what matters most when evaluating a platform like this?

🌟 Enterprise value if they want to go deeper

  • Brand Governance — Lock down templates, fonts, logos globally. Prevent off-brand content before it’s published.
  • Operational Efficiency — Any team creates professional content without bottlenecks. Replaces PowerPoint, Adobe, video tools, docs — one platform.
  • Security & Compliance — SSO, SCIM, ISO 27001, audit logs, Canva Shield AI indemnity.
  • Scalable Collaboration — Distribute and manage branded content org-wide. Multi-team, multi-region.
📈 Forrester independently validated Canva Enterprise: 414% ROI payback < 6 months. Use this if they ask for proof of value.

🚫 Handling Objections

“The team is happy with the free version.”
“That’s a good sign — it means they’re getting value. The free tier is built for individuals though. What you’re missing is the layer that makes it work for a team: brand controls, admin visibility, premium content, and proper invoicing. Most teams that upgrade say they wish they’d done it earlier.”
“We weren’t planning to expand this.”
“Totally fair. I’m not asking you to expand it — I’m asking you to formalise what’s already happening. The usage is there. The question is whether it’s sitting on personal cards or running through SHI properly. The latter is a 30-minute conversation.”
“We already use Adobe for design.”
“Adobe’s great for creative professionals. Canva fills a different gap — it empowers everyone else on the team who needs to create content but isn’t a designer. Most companies use both. The question is: how are your non-designers creating content today?”
“What’s the minimum commitment?”
“For Canva Business, it’s 25 seats on an annual basis — under $5k total, credit card, done same day. For Enterprise, minimum is typically 30 seats on an annual agreement through SHI. Happy to get Canva’s team to scope out exactly what that looks like for your org.”

📋 Closing

Business close “The simplest next step is a 20-minute call with Canva’s team — they can scope the Business agreement based on your current usage and have something to you within days. What’s the best email to send the calendar invite?”
Enterprise close “Based on what you’ve described, I think you’re actually an Enterprise conversation. Canva’s team can put together a proposal based on your team size, usage, and requirements — usually within a week. Worth a 20-minute scoping call?”
