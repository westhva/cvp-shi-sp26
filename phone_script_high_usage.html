🔥 Phone Script — HOT & WARM Accounts

SHI × Canva Spring 2026 Blitz  ·  These accounts already use Canva — move them to paid

⚡ Also Offer: Canva Business for Smaller Teams

If the account is smaller or a manager wants a quick win: Canva Business (25 seats) is currently under $5,000 total — credit card purchase, no procurement needed. Price increase is already published online. Worth mentioning if Enterprise feels like too big a conversation right now.

💳 <$5k instant buy ▲ Price increase coming ⚡ Faster close

📄 Before You Call

  • Check YAU and paid seat count in the dashboard — these inform which script to use
  • HOT = 10+ paid seats already. WARM = high free usage. Both are warm calls — they know Canva
  • Try to reach an IT manager, procurement lead, or the team that manages software spend
  • Goal: get them to move billing through SHI or commit to a formal agreement

📞 Opening

Say this “Hey [Name], my name’s [Your Name] from SHI — I’m one of your account reps. I’m reaching out because I can see your organisation is already using Canva, and I wanted to talk about how we can formalise that through SHI and unlock some additional features. Is now an okay time?”

🔥 HOT Accounts — 10+ Paid Seats

🔥 Use this script “I can see your team already has paid Canva seats — which is great. The reason I’m calling is that right now those are individual subscriptions sitting outside your normal procurement flow.

Moving them through SHI gets you consolidated invoicing and POs, proper software asset management, and unlocks the Enterprise tier — which adds SSO, SCIM provisioning, brand kits, admin controls, and SOC 2 compliance.

In most cases, the per-seat cost stays similar or comes down once you’re on a formal agreement. Would it make sense to get a quick call set up with Canva’s team to scope this out?”
If the team is small (<50 users), you can also mention Canva Business as a faster option: under $5k for 25 seats, no SSO/SCIM needed, instant credit card purchase.

🌊 WARM Accounts — High Free Usage

🌊 Use this script “I can see your team has been active on Canva — a good chunk of your users are on the free tier right now. The reason I’m calling is that there’s a real gap between what they’re using and what they could be doing.

The paid tier unlocks premium templates, brand kits, background remover, video editing, and admin controls — plus your content stays on-brand across the whole team. And right now there’s a good window to lock in pricing before an increase that’s already been published.

Would it make sense to grab 20 minutes with Canva’s team to look at what a formal agreement would look like for your org?”
Canva Business angle works well here too if they’re SMB-sized — simpler conversation, faster decision, under $5k.

🌟 Key Value Props (if they want more)

  • Enterprise controls — SSO, SCIM, admin dashboard, brand management, approval workflows
  • Tool consolidation — replaces PowerPoint, Adobe, video tools, and social design in one platform
  • Faster content — teams create professional content in minutes, not days. No designer required
  • SOC 2 / GDPR compliant — enterprise-grade security already built in
  • Procurement simplicity — one invoice through SHI, not scattered individual credit card charges

🚫 Handling Objections

“The team is happy with the free version.”
“That’s a good sign — it means they’re getting value. The paid tier is where the real productivity gains are: brand kits, premium content, video editing, and admin controls so IT has visibility. Most teams that upgrade say they wish they’d done it sooner.”
“We weren’t planning to expand this.”
“Totally understand. The main reason I’m calling now is timing — a price increase is already live on Canva’s website, so locking in before it takes effect makes sense. Even a quick discovery call with Canva’s team could be worth it.”
“Who decides on software spend here?”
“Great question — is that IT, procurement, or a department head? I’d love to make sure the right person has the info. If it helps, I can send a one-pager to pass along.”
“What’s the minimum commitment?”
“For Enterprise, the minimum is typically 30 seats on an annual agreement through SHI. If you want something quicker and smaller, Canva Business starts at 25 seats and is currently under $5,000 total — which most teams can put on a card same day.”

📋 Closing

Ask for the next step “Does it make sense to get a 20-minute call in the diary with Canva’s team? They can put together a proposal based on your current usage and walk through what a formal agreement looks like. What’s the best email to send the calendar invite to?”
If not right now “No problem. I’ll send you a quick note with the key info — including the pricing before the increase — so you have it when the time’s right. Thanks, [Name]!”
