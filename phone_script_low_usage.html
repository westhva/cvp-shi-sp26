🚀 Phone Script — POTENTIAL & NEW Accounts

SHI × Canva Spring 2026 Blitz  ·  Low or zero Canva usage — educate first, sell second

Strategy for this tier

These accounts don’t have a Canva footprint yet. Don’t lead with product features or price. Lead with their problem: how does their team create content today, and how much is that costing them? When they describe the pain — designers as bottlenecks, off-brand content, slow turnaround — that’s your opening. Companies without existing Canva usage almost always end up on Enterprise once they understand the workflow play.

🔍 Discover the pain → 🌟 Connect to workflows → 📈 Enterprise CTA

📄 Before You Call

  • Low YAU = they may have tried Canva and not adopted it, or never evaluated it at all
  • Don’t assume disinterest — assume unawareness. Most people still think Canva is a free design tool for social posts.
  • Target: marketing manager, ops lead, department head, or anyone who owns content creation workflows
  • Your job on this call is to ask great questions and listen — not pitch
  • DO NOT mention price increases or urgency — irrelevant to a non-user and will kill the call

📞 Opening

Say this “Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] from SHI. Quick question — how does your team currently create content? Things like presentations, social posts, marketing materials, internal comms?”
💡 Starting with a question, not a pitch, changes the whole dynamic. Let them talk. You’re diagnosing, not presenting. Whatever they say next tells you everything.
If they say "we have a design team" “Got it — and when other teams need something created quickly, do they wait for the design team, or do they try to make it themselves?”
If they say "we use PowerPoint / Google Slides / Adobe" “Totally — those are great for certain things. I’m curious though: when someone outside the creative team needs to produce something on-brand — a sales deck, a regional flyer, a quick social post — how does that typically go?”

🔍 Discovery — Find the Real Pain

Your goal is to get them describing a workflow problem in their own words. These are the signals you’re listening for:

Designer is a bottleneck Off-brand content going out Slow turnaround times Too many tools Regional teams doing their own thing Non-designers trying to create
🔍 SPICED discovery questions
  • Situation: How many people in your org regularly need to produce or update content — not just the design team?
  • Pain: What’s the biggest friction point when someone outside of design needs something created quickly?
  • Impact: If every team could produce professional, on-brand content without going through a bottleneck — what would that change for you?
  • Critical Event: Is there an upcoming campaign, launch, or initiative where faster content creation would matter most?
  • Decision: If a solution addressed those problems, what would the evaluation process look like on your end?
💡 You don’t need to ask all five. Get them talking, pick the ones that fit the conversation. The goal is to have them articulate the problem — not for you to tell it to them.

🎉 Connecting the Pain to Canva Enterprise

Once you have their pain in their own words, bridge to the platform. Don’t list features — mirror their language back.

📈 The workflow reframe “What you’re describing is actually exactly where Canva Enterprise plays. Most people think of Canva as a design tool — and it is — but for organisations, the bigger value is what it does to your workflows.

It gives every team — marketing, sales, ops, HR, regional offices — the ability to create professional, on-brand content without touching a designer. Your brand kit locks down the templates, fonts, and logos so nothing goes out off-brand. Your design team sets the guardrails once, and then gets out of the bottleneck permanently.

Imagine your sales team updating their own decks. Your regional managers creating their own event flyers. Your HR team producing their own onboarding docs — all locked to your brand. That’s what we’re talking about.”
📈 The consolidation angle (if they use multiple tools) “One thing that surprises most people: Canva Enterprise replaces a lot of what they’re already paying for. Presentations, social content, video, docs, whiteboards, PDFs — it’s one platform. Most organisations find they can retire 2–3 other subscriptions once the team is fully onboarded.

Forrester independently benchmarked this: organisations using Canva Enterprise saw 414% ROI and got their investment back in under 6 months. That’s not a marketing claim — that’s an independent audit.”

🌟 Enterprise value — if they want to go deeper

  • Brand Governance — Templates, fonts, logos locked globally. Every team creates on-brand without design review.
  • Operational Efficiency — Any team creates professional content without bottlenecks. One platform replaces PowerPoint, Adobe, video tools, and docs.
  • Security & Compliance — SSO, SCIM provisioning, ISO 27001, SOC 2, audit logs, Canva Shield AI indemnity.
  • Scalable Collaboration — Multi-team, multi-region. Distribute and manage branded content at scale.
📈 Forrester TEI: 414% ROI payback < 6 months. Lead with this when they ask “how do we justify the cost?”

🚫 Handling Objections

“We already have a design team — we don’t need this.”
“Your design team is probably the best reason to get it. Right now they’re probably spending a lot of time on requests that don’t need their skill level — small edits, quick resizes, updating slide decks. Canva offloads that work to the people making the requests, so your design team can focus on the work that actually needs them.”
“We use Adobe / PowerPoint / Google Slides already.”
“Those are great tools for specific people. The difference with Canva is that it’s built for everyone — not just the people who are trained on design software. The question isn’t whether your design team can use Adobe. It’s whether your sales team, your ops team, your regional managers can create on-brand content without going through a bottleneck. That’s the gap Canva fills.”
“We don’t have budget for a new tool right now.”
“That’s fair — timing matters. I’d just push back slightly: most orgs find that Canva Enterprise replaces tools they’re already paying for. If it can consolidate your presentation software, design subscriptions, and content tools under one contract, the net cost is often lower than what they’re spending today. Worth a 20-minute conversation just to run the numbers?”
“We tried it before and the team didn’t adopt it.”
“That’s the most common thing I hear, and it’s almost always the same reason — it was rolled out without templates set up, without brand kit, without a clear use case. Enterprise is a different product. Canva’s team sets up your brand kit, pre-builds your templates, and runs onboarding with your team. The organisations that see 400%+ ROI didn’t just hand over a licence — they had a proper rollout.”
“I’m not sure we have enough people to justify Enterprise.”
“Enterprise starts at 30 seats — so it’s not just for large organisations. And the value isn’t about the number of users, it’s about the teams that benefit from having a structured, governed content platform. Happy to get Canva’s team to scope out what that looks like for your specific situation.”

📋 Closing

Enterprise close “Based on what you’ve described — [echo their pain back] — I think it’s worth a proper conversation with Canva’s team. They can put together a proposal based on your team structure and use case. Typically takes about 20 minutes on a scoping call, and you’d have a proposal within a week.

What’s the best email to send you a calendar invite?”
If they’re not ready to meet “No problem. Can I send you a quick summary of what we talked about — the workflow efficiency angle and the Forrester numbers? That way you have something to share internally if the conversation comes up. What’s your email?”
