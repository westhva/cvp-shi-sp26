📄 Before You Call

  • Look up the company briefly — industry, size, what they do
  • Aim for Marketing, Operations, HR, or IT
  • These accounts have little or no Canva usage — this is a discovery and education call
  • Goal: get a meeting or a “send me more info” — not a close on the first call

📞 Opening

Say this “Hey [Name], my name’s [Your Name] from SHI. I’m reaching out to a handful of our SMB customers about a Canva promotion we’re running — wanted to see if it might be a fit for your team. Do you have 60 seconds?”
If they ask “what’s Canva?” — great! Zero competition. Go straight to the Business pitch below.

⚡ Canva Business Pitch — Lead With This

🔥 Main pitch “We’re working with Canva on a limited-time offer for SMB customers. Canva Business — 25 seats — is currently priced just under $5,000 total. For most IT teams, that’s a credit card purchase. No procurement process, no waiting.

The reason I’m calling now is that Canva has already published a price increase online. Once that kicks in, this goes above the $5k mark — and the conversation looks different. So the window to lock in at this price is short.

It replaces presentations, documents, social content, video editing — all in one tool anyone on the team can use from day one, no design experience needed. Would it make sense to grab a quick 20-minute call with Canva’s team to see if it fits?”
If they need SSO or SCIM, pivot to Enterprise. But Business is the easy first conversation for SMB — simple, fast, under the threshold.

🌟 What Canva Replaces (if they want more detail)

Platform overview “Think about every time your team makes a presentation, a document, a social post, a video, or a flyer. Right now that probably means jumping between PowerPoint, Google Slides, Adobe, and whatever else people have installed on their machines.

Canva puts all of that in one platform — with brand controls so everything looks consistent — and people are creating in minutes, not days.”

🚫 Handling Objections

“We already have PowerPoint / Google Slides.”
“Most of our customers do — and they keep using them. What Canva adds is a design layer on top: on-brand templates, social and video formats, and a platform everyone on the team can actually use without a designer. A lot of teams end up replacing 3 or 4 tools.”
“We’re not really a design-heavy company.”
“That’s actually where Canva fits best — teams without dedicated designers. It’s built so anyone can make something professional-looking in minutes. Marketing, HR, operations, sales — all use it.”
“$5k still needs sign-off.”
“Totally fair. This is one of the lowest-threshold software decisions out there — and the price goes up soon, so the timing works in your favour. Worth a quick conversation before the window closes?”
“We’re not looking at new software right now.”
“I get it — timing isn’t always right. The main reason I’m calling now is the price point. Once it goes up, this conversation looks different. Even a 20-minute call before then could be worth it.”
“Send me an email instead.”
“Absolutely — what’s the best address? I’ll send something short with the current pricing and a link to book time directly with Canva’s team if it looks interesting.”

📋 Closing the Call

Ask for the meeting “Based on what you’ve heard, does it make sense to grab 20 minutes with Canva’s team? They’ll show you exactly what it looks like for a team your size and answer any questions. No pressure — just a look.”
If yes “Perfect. I’ll send over a calendar link right now. What’s the best email?”
If not right now “No problem. I’ll drop you a note with the pricing — worth having on file before the increase. Thanks for your time, [Name]!”
